## Original Wix Page Logic

The old Wix page logic appears to be a sparse photo gallery, not a case-study narrative. The page likely used very little written copy and relied on large image placement, generous margins, and alternating aspect ratios.

The strongest logic is:

- Lead with a tall, centered image of dark birds on a sloped roof or thatched surface. This works as a visual signature because it has strong diagonals, texture, and negative space.
- Follow with wide, quiet landscape crops of soccer goals, trees, and winter field edges. These images are not informational; they create pacing and atmosphere.
- Insert smaller or paired images such as the architectural silhouette and black-and-white animal/figure scene as visual pauses.
- Use the night storefront as a cinematic wide image near the end because it introduces human presence and light contrast.
- Keep captions minimal or absent. If captions are added, they should describe the image plainly rather than turn the page into an essay.

For the rebuild, this means the page should be image-led, low-copy, and composed like a wall of selected photographs. Do not add process sections, metrics, or a long hero explanation.
