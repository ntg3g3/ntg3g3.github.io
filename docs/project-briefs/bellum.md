Confirmed from `social-feed.webp`: players view posts in a social media group ("Animal Lovers"), including a claim about Booker Dewitt, comments, likes, and share buttons.

Confirmed from `strategy-map.webp`: a design board says "Create a discord between two people in this company to successfully crumble this division of the company" and "This would help Bellum reach its goal as this company stands in between them." Confirmed from `plant-success.webp` / `plant-failure.webp`: actions can succeed or fail and award influence / shares.

Inferred core loop: inspect available information and characters, choose a channel or location to plant / amplify information, watch social response through feed or scene feedback, and manipulate relationships / polarization toward a strategic goal.

## Original Wix Page Logic

The original Bellum page appears to be a systems-heavy design page. It does not simply show final art. It begins with research / reference material and possibly technical or design notes, then moves into UI wireframes and interaction boards. The middle of the page is where the real portfolio value sits: social feed UI, strategy map, information cards, rage level, and success / failure feedback. Later images provide worldbuilding: rally, street, shop, dialogue, and portraits.

The layout logic should therefore be: topic / premise first, system diagram second, social feed and action feedback third, narrative world last. If the page leads with too many scene images, the viewer misses that Bellum is about modeling information flow and social manipulation.

Large images should be `strategy-map.webp` and `social-feed.webp`, because they contain readable rules and mechanics. `plant-success.webp` and `plant-failure.webp` should be displayed side by side as a binary feedback pair. Character portraits should be small; they support the system but should not dominate.
